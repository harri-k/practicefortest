First Microprocessor

  • Intel's 4004 microprocessor marked the beginning of microcomputing, leading to the development of personal computers.A microprocessor is a crucial component of modern computing devices, serving as the central processing unit (CPU) responsible for executing instructions and performing calculations. It consists of millions or even billions of transistors etched onto a silicon chip, and its primary function is to carry out tasks required for the operation of computers, smartphones, and various other electronic devices. Microprocessors have evolved significantly since their inception, becoming smaller, more powerful, and energy-efficient, driving the advancement of technology across various industries.
