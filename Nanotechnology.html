Nanotechnology and Beyond

    • Nanotechnology pushed the limits of chip miniaturization, enabling more transistors and greater computational power.Key aspects of nanotechnology include the ability to manipulate matter at the atomic and molecular level, leading to the creation of new materials with unique properties and applications. Some notable examples of nanotechnology applications include nanomedicine (using nanoparticles for targeted drug delivery and medical imaging), nanoelectronics (developing smaller and more efficient electronic components), and nanomaterials (creating lightweight and durable materials with enhanced properties).
