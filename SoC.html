About System on a Chip (SoC)

A System on a Chip (SoC) integrates multiple electronic components required for a computing or electronic system onto a single integrated circuit (IC). It combines various functionalities such as a processor, memory, input/output interfaces, and sometimes additional components like GPU, DSP, and connectivity modules onto a single chip. SoCs are commonly used in smartphones, tablets, smart appliances, IoT devices, and other embedded systems due to their compact size and efficiency.

SoCs come in different sizes and architectures, ranging from small form factors for wearable devices to larger chips used in high-performance computing. Their features include low power consumption, high performance, and versatility, making them essential for the development of advanced technological devices.

The appearance of SoCs varies based on the manufacturer and intended application. Some SoCs come in packages with integrated heat sinks or cooling solutions to manage heat dissipation.
